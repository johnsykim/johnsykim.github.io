Analysis of an Antimicrobial Resistance Transmission Mode​l
Pomona College Mathematics Department  |  Advisor: Dr. Adolfo J. Rumbos
May 2017 --- Sep 2019  |  Claremont, CA, USA
  • Conducted independent mathematics research on a compartmental model of AMR
  • Published in Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics (SIAM) Undergraduate Research Online
  • Featured on SIAM's curation of 170+ peer-reviewed papers on disease modeling --- SIAM Epidemiology Collection
  • Awarded Kenneth Cooke Summer Research Fellowship ($4,000)
  • [paper]
​We present an analysis of a system of differential equations that models the transmission dynamics of pathogens with antimicrobial resistance (AMR) in an intensive care unit. We first present a stability analysis of the limiting system and prove the existence of a locally asymptotically stable equilibrium point under a set of constraints expressed in terms of reproductive numbers. We then proceed to an asymptotic analysis of the non–autonomous, two–dimensional system by applying a Poincaré–Bendixson type trichotomy result proved by Thieme (1992, 1994). In particular, we establish that any forward bounded trajectory of the non–autonomous system that starts within a defined rectangular region will converge toward the equilibrium point of the limiting system, provided that certain conditions given in terms of the reproductive numbers are satisfied.
Measuring Import Substitution in Botswana Poultry Industry
Pomona College Economics Department  |  Advisor: Dr. Pierangelo De Pace
Jan 2016 --- Aug 2016  |  Gaborone, BOTSWANA
  • Conducted independent economics research on agricultural protectionism in Botswana
  • Constructed an econometric model to gauge cross-sectoral policy impacts on agricultural industries subject to protectionism
  • Interviewed Botswana's businessmen, economists, and policymakers to gather firsthand qualitative data
  • Presented at Pomona College's annual academic conference attended by 300+ community members
  • Awarded Mellon Presidential Leadership Ashe Africa Summer Grant ($5,000)
  • [proposal] [poster]
With high level of trade freedom and long history of trade liberalization, one would expect Botswana to have one of the most open economies in the world. A look into its economy, however, reveals that Botswana has had import substitution (denoted IS hereafter) in place for some major sectors. The most notable case is the poultry sector in which there existed support for small scale local producers and state sponsored marketing channels. As was often the case in post-colonial African agriculture, these IS policies were implemented with good intentions yet have turned the industry into a legal oligopoly. It is this observation of sectoral development in a small developing economy that motivates us to consider both qualitative and quantitative data of IS in Botswana poultry industry.​
Investigation on the Management Status of K-Pop Revenue Model and Finding Ways for Improvement
Yonsei University  |  Advisor: Dr. Soonkyoo Choe
Jun 2012 --- Aug 2012  |  Seoul, KOREA
  • [paper]
  • Accepted to International Journal of Trade, Economics and Finance
Korean Wave, the significant increase in the popularity of South Korean entertainment and culture, is sweeping across the whole world. One of its biggest contributors is music, often designated as K-Pop, and when this factor is studied critically, the art world can realize that therein lie problems with its revenue model and propose possible solutions. In order to make K-Pop a sustainable growth engine of Korean Wave, the music industry needs to be undertaking various initiatives at a domestic level. The aim of this research is to determine the state of affairs regarding K-Pop revenue model and review possible solutions which will help readers to gain insight into one particular sector of the world economy.
The History of Sicilian Mafia from an Economic Perspective
Hankuk Academy of Foreign Studies  |  Advisor: Eric Moberg
Jan 2012 --- Jun 2012  |  Seoul, KOREA
  • [paper]
  • Accepted to International Journal of Arts and Sciences (IJAS)
  • Presented at the 2012 IJAS Toronto Conference hosted by Ryerson University
The Mafia, an association of criminal groups that share a common organizational structure and code of conduct, committed all kinds of crimes to pursue what it wants. For decades, this syndicate has broadened the boundaries of activities, from existing crimes such as drug and arms dealings, human trafficking, and forgery, to new kinds of crimes like bankruptcy fraud and political corruption. Based on this change in identity, the paper deals with: (i) the name and the concept, (ii) roots, and (iii) the modern Mafia as a business enterprise. By understanding the factors which have shaped the modern form of Sicilian Mafia, people will be able to gain insight to one particular sector of the world economy.
