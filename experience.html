"Dually eligible beneficiaries receive both Medicare and Medicaid benefits by virtue of age or disability and low income. The design of the programs creates particular challenges for efficient, effective health care delivery. The existence of separate funding streams can create barriers to coordination of care for dually eligible beneficiaries, which in turn can lead to increased costs and poor health outcomes. These issues are of particular concern given the high health needs of dually eligible beneficiaries and the fact that this group accounts for a disproportionate share of both Medicare and Medicaid spending."

The current Chinese healthcare system is simply not well-functioning. On one hand, patients must endure infamously long wait times just to secure a doctor’s appointment. Even the patients with common ailments unquestioningly head to big hospitals instead of community health centers where they can be treated far more effectively and efficiently by a GP. On the other hand, doctors face a massive load of patients with low health literacy and distrust. More than 70 percent of doctors in China have suffered verbal abuse or physical violence, and more than 60 percent of doctors do not want their children to follow in their footsteps because of the excessively heavy workload and lack of a sense of security.
As a Health Systems intern, I found empirical research to be crucial in raising awareness. There is strong international evidence that community health services staffed by well-trained GPs are the most effective and efficient means of providing good quality health care to the whole population. My team strove to design a campaign that initiates the conversation, offers credible information, and provides an avenue for further action. We considered the following questions: “Why should Chinese people care about GPs? How can we encourage the public but not mislead them?” What came to my mind were less known benefits of GPs, such as faster appointments and referrals, more accessible medication, and less out-of-pocket medical fees. I realized how empirical research could help healthcare experts develop key messages and select target audiences.
